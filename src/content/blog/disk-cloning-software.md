Disk cloning creates a sector-by-sector copy of an entire drive, including the operating system, applications, and data. It's the most reliable way to migrate to a new SSD or create a bootable backup that can be deployed instantly if your primary drive fails.

We compared six popular cloning tools: Macrium Reflect, Acronis True Image, Clonezilla, Samsung Data Migration, EaseUS Todo Backup, and AOMEI Backupper. Each was tested for cloning speed, boot reliability after clone, and support for different partition schemes including GPT and MBR.

Macrium Reflect delivered the most consistent results, successfully creating bootable clones in every scenario we tested. Its intelligent sector copy mode skips empty blocks, cutting clone times by up to 60% compared to raw sector-by-sector copies. Acronis True Image matched Macrium in reliability and added cloud backup integration, though at a higher price point.

For budget-conscious users, Clonezilla remains the gold standard in free cloning software. While its text-based interface can be intimidating, it supports virtually every file system and partition layout. Samsung Data Migration is worth mentioning for Samsung SSD owners — it's free, simple, and optimized for Samsung drives, though it won't work with other brands.

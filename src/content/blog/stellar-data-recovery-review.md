Stellar Data Recovery has established itself as one of the most recognized names in the data recovery industry, offering separate products for Windows and Mac with a range of licensing tiers. We put the Professional edition through our standard test suite to see how it performs against the competition.

Installation is straightforward, and the interface is clean and intuitive with a wizard-based workflow that guides you through selecting file types, choosing a drive, and starting the scan. The quick scan completed in under two minutes on our 500GB test drive, while the deep scan took approximately 45 minutes — both reasonable times that compare favorably to competing tools.

Recovery performance was strong across the board. Stellar recovered 91% of deleted files in our standard test, including documents, photos, and compressed archives. It particularly excelled at media recovery, successfully reconstructing fragmented video files that some competitors missed. The preview feature is well-implemented, letting you verify files before recovery.

On the downside, Stellar's pricing is on the higher end at $79.99 per year for the Professional edition, and it uses a subscription model rather than a perpetual license. The free version limits recovery to 1GB, which is useful for evaluation but inadequate for real recovery scenarios. Overall, Stellar is a reliable choice for users who want a polished experience and strong media recovery capabilities, though budget-conscious users may find better value elsewhere.

Recuva by Piriform has been one of the most recognizable names in free data recovery since its initial release. The free version offers unlimited recovery with no file size caps, which is rare in today's market where most competitors lock advanced features behind paywalls.

In our testing, Recuva performed reasonably well for simple deletion recovery on NTFS drives, finding about 78% of recently deleted files. However, it struggled with deep scan scenarios such as formatted drives and corrupted partitions, where newer tools like Disk Drill and PhotoRec significantly outperformed it. The interface also feels dated compared to modern competitors.

One notable limitation is the lack of macOS support — Recuva remains Windows-only. It also doesn't support newer file systems like APFS or ext4, which limits its usefulness in mixed-OS environments. The deep scan mode is slow, taking nearly three times longer than comparable tools on the same test drive.

For basic file recovery on Windows, Recuva remains a solid free option that gets the job done. But if you're dealing with a complex data loss scenario or need cross-platform support, investing in a more capable tool is worth the cost. The professional version at $19.95 adds virtual hard drive support but doesn't significantly improve recovery rates.

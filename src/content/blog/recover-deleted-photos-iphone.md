Losing precious photos from your iPhone can be stressful, but Apple provides several built-in recovery options before you need to resort to third-party tools. The key is knowing where to look and acting before the recovery window closes.

Your first stop should be the Recently Deleted album in the Photos app. Apple keeps deleted photos here for 30 days before permanently removing them. Simply open Photos, tap Albums, scroll to Utilities, and check Recently Deleted. If your photos are there, select them and tap Recover. This works even if you deleted the photos weeks ago, as long as the 30-day window hasn't expired.

If the photos aren't in Recently Deleted, check your iCloud account. Sign in to iCloud.com, go to Photos, and look in the Recently Deleted folder there — iCloud maintains its own 30-day retention independently. You can also restore from an iCloud or iTunes backup, though this will replace your current phone data with the backup's contents unless you use a selective backup extraction tool.

For photos that have been permanently deleted beyond the 30-day window, third-party tools like iMobie PhoneRescue or Tenorshare UltData can scan your iPhone or backup files for recoverable data. Success rates vary and depend heavily on how much new data has been written to the device since deletion. The sooner you attempt recovery, the better your chances.

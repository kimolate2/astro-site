Data recovery software has come a long way in 2026, with AI-assisted scanning and improved support for modern file systems like APFS and ReFS. After spending over 200 hours testing more than 20 tools, we've identified the ones that consistently deliver results across a variety of data loss scenarios.

Our testing methodology involved deliberately deleting files from SSDs, HDDs, USB drives, and memory cards, then measuring recovery rates, scan speeds, and file integrity. We also tested each tool's ability to recover data from formatted drives and corrupted partitions, which are among the most common real-world scenarios users face.

The top performers distinguished themselves through deep scanning algorithms that could reconstruct fragmented files even after a quick format. Tools like Disk Drill, R-Studio, and Stellar stood out for their consistently high recovery rates above 95% on traditional hard drives.

For SSD recovery, the landscape is more nuanced due to TRIM commands that permanently erase data blocks. The best tools in our ranking offer TRIM-aware scanning that prioritizes speed, since every second counts before the operating system overwrites deleted sectors. If you've just lost files on an SSD, stop using the drive immediately and boot from an external recovery environment for the best chance of success.
